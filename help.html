✅ What to Expect

Seeking help for the first time can feel overwhelming. Here's what typically happens:

1
Call or walk in

Most emergency services accept walk-ins. You don't need an appointment for crisis shelter or food.

2
You don't need ID

Emergency shelter and many food programs do not require identification. Bring it if you have it, but don't let lack of ID stop you from seeking help.

3
A case manager will help you

Staff at most shelters can connect you to housing, food, health, and legal services — all from one place.

4
Everything is confidential

Your information is not shared without your consent. Crisis hotlines are fully anonymous.
