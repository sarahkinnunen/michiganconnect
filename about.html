About The Connect

Our mission, how we work, and our commitment to accuracy and community.

Our Mission

The Connect is a free, public-facing resource hub designed to help young people — and anyone in need — quickly find accurate, up-to-date information about critical services in Wayne and Monroe counties, Michigan.

We believe access to housing, food, healthcare, legal help, and crisis support is a human right. No one should struggle to find these services because of a difficult website, outdated information, or a design that wasn't built for people under stress.

The Connect is built with dignity in mind — for people experiencing homelessness, food insecurity, mental health crises, and other challenges. Every design decision prioritizes simplicity, speed, and clarity.

Our Values

🎯 Accessibility First

Our site meets WCAG 2.1 AA standards. Large text, high contrast, and keyboard navigation ensure everyone can use it, regardless of ability or device.

✅ Verified Information

Every resource in our database includes a last verified date. We regularly review and update listings, and encourage the community to report outdated information.

🔒 Privacy by Default

We do not collect personal data from visitors. The site uses no tracking cookies, no analytics third parties, and no user accounts.

🌐 Open Data

Our resource data is available for download in open formats. We encourage other organizations to use and build on this data to serve their communities.

🤝 Youth-Centered

The Connect is designed with and for young people — particularly those ages 16–24 facing housing instability, foster care transitions, or other life challenges.

📍 Local Focus, Expandable

We start with Wayne and Monroe counties. Our data architecture is designed to scale to additional Michigan counties as the project grows.

How We Verify Data

We understand that outdated resource information can cause real harm — someone in crisis may show up at a location that has moved or closed. That's why accuracy is non-negotiable.

  1. Each resource is researched from primary sources (official organization websites, direct phone calls).
  2. A last_verified_date is recorded for every entry.
  3. Community members can report outdated information using our contact form.
  4. All suggested resources are reviewed before publication.
  5. Resources not verified within 12 months are marked for re-verification and temporarily flagged.
